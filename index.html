Dual Pixel Autofocus

Dual Pixel Autofocus is a Canon technology used in many of their DSLR and mirrorless cameras. It provides fast and accurate autofocus by using phase-detection pixels on the sensor itself. This is especially useful for video recording and live view shooting, as it allows for smooth and precise focusing with minimal lag.

Lens Quality and Focal Length

The lens quality and focal length determine how well the camera can capture images. A higher-quality lens offers better sharpness, contrast, and color accuracy. Focal length, measured in millimeters (mm), affects the camera's zoom capability and field of view. Wide-angle lenses are good for landscapes, while telephoto lenses are suited for distant subjects.

Shutter Speed and Aperture

Shutter speed controls how long the camera's sensor is exposed to light, affecting motion blur and exposure. Faster shutter speeds freeze motion, while slower speeds can create motion blur. Aperture, represented by f-stop numbers (e.g., f/2.8, f/5.6), controls the amount of light entering the lens and the depth of field. A lower f-stop number allows more light and a shallower depth of field, while a higher number results in less light and a greater depth of field.
